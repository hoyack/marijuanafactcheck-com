Claims graded by evidence

Every claim is evaluated against published research. Grades reflect source quality, replication, and effect size. Uncertainty is labeled, not hidden.

CBD reduces seizure frequency in Dravet syndrome

Randomized controlled trial evidence with replication across multiple centers. FDA-approved Epidiolex formulation.

BModerate

THC provides short-term pain relief for chronic neuropathic pain

Multiple RCTs with moderate effect sizes; heterogeneity in dosing and delivery methods limits precision of effect estimate.

BModerate

Nabiximols (Sativex) reduces spasticity in multiple sclerosis

Multiple RCTs and real-world evidence support moderate effect. Approved in 25+ countries; not FDA-approved in the US.

5 sourcesModerate confidenceLast reviewed: 2024View sources

CLimited

Cannabis effectively treats anxiety disorders

Limited RCT data. Some observational studies suggest short-term anxiolytic effects; others show anxiogenic effects at higher doses. CBD-only formulations studied separately.

4 sourcesLow confidenceLast reviewed: 2023View sources

DInsufficient

Cannabis improves sleep quality across populations

Conflicting evidence. Short-term subjective sleep improvements reported; objective polysomnography shows reduced REM sleep. Long-term effects understudied.

6 sourcesLow confidenceLast reviewed: 2024View sources

FContradicted

Cannabis "cures" cancer

No human RCT evidence. Preclinical cell-line and animal studies do not constitute treatment evidence. Multiple systematic reviews find no support for this claim.
