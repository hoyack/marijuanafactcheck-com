# One-page concept brief

## Audience and problem
Primary audience: journalists, policy analysts, healthcare communicators, and evidence-literate adults (21+) who encounter cannabis claims in news, social media, product marketing, or policy debate and need a structured, citable evaluation of the evidence behind them. The problem: cannabis claims circulate widely with no standardized evidence grading, creating confusion between supported findings, preliminary research, and outright misinformation.

## Positioning
Marijuana Fact Check is the citation-first evidence database for cannabis claims: every claim gets a letter grade, every grade cites its sources, every uncertainty is labeled. It is not a lifestyle publication, product review site, or advocacy platform.

## Information architecture
- Home: searchable claim database preview, evidence grade explainer, newsletter CTA
- Claims: filterable, searchable list of evaluated claims with grade badges and confidence bars
- Methodology: transparent grading rubric, confidence levels, source hierarchy, correction policy
- Sources: evidence hierarchy and source register with DOIs
- Newsletter: spam-protected Netlify Forms signup for evidence updates
- Submit Claim: spam-protected research queue intake form
- Corrections, Disclosure, Privacy, Terms: trust and compliance pages

## Visual direction
A clinical research report: navy ink on off-white paper, evidence blue for action and trust, safety green for strong grades, amber for uncertainty warnings, red for medical/legal boundaries and contradicted claims. Monospace labels and grade badges create a "lab report" feel. No cannabis leaf imagery, no lifestyle photography, no green-washed aesthetics.

## Content pillars
1. Evidence grading: structured, transparent, reproducible claim evaluation
2. Source transparency: every citation traceable to published research
3. Uncertainty labeling: confidence levels and limitations stated explicitly
4. Corrections: dated, public, never silent
5. Medical/legal boundaries: clear disclaimers, no treatment advice

## CTA and revenue hypothesis
Primary conversion: `newsletter_subscribed` via the evidence newsletter signup. Secondary: `claim_submitted` via the research queue. Revenue hypothesis: sponsored research reports (clearly labeled, editorially separated), institutional access, and carefully disclosed grants — launched only after audience and trust are established. No cannabis product advertising, affiliate revenue, or brand sponsorships.

## Safety boundary
Adults 21+ only. Not medical advice. Not legal advice. No product recommendations. No dosage guidance. No treatment claims. Evidence grades summarize published research only.
