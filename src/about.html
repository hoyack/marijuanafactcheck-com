About Marijuana Fact Check

Our Mission

Marijuana Fact Check exists to bring evidence-based clarity to cannabis discourse. In a landscape dominated by marketing, anecdotes, and outdated science, we provide a single, trustworthy source that grades claims by evidence quality, links to primary sources, and clearly separates fact from speculation.

We are non-partisan. We have no financial stake in cannabis companies. Our only commitment is to the evidence — wherever it leads.

How We Grade Claims

Every claim receives an evidence grade based on the strength, quality, and quantity of available research:

A
Strong Evidence

Multiple high-quality, peer-reviewed studies with consistent results. Meta-analyses or systematic reviews support the claim.

B
Moderate Evidence

Several well-designed studies support the claim, but some inconsistency or methodological limitations exist.

C
Limited Evidence

Few studies, small sample sizes, or primarily preclinical (animal/cell) research. Plausible but unconfirmed in humans.

D
Weak Evidence

Anecdotal reports, expert opinion only, or studies with significant flaws. Low confidence in the claim.

F
Debunked / No Evidence

Claim is contradicted by available evidence, or no credible research exists to support it.

Verdict Categories

In addition to evidence grades, each claim receives a plain-language verdict:

  • Mostly True — Available evidence strongly supports the claim.
  • Mixed Evidence — Evidence points in multiple directions; no clear conclusion.
  • Mostly False — Weight of evidence contradicts the claim.
  • False — Claim is contradicted by strong evidence.
  • Unproven — Insufficient research exists to evaluate the claim.

Uncertainty Rating

Every claim includes an uncertainty rating (Low / Medium / High) reflecting:

  • Quality and size of available studies
  • Consistency across research findings
  • Recency of evidence
  • Applicability to human populations

We err on the side of disclosing uncertainty. "We don't know yet" is an honest answer.

Editorial Standards

  • Primary sources only. We cite peer-reviewed studies, government data, and official reports — not other fact-check sites or news summaries.
  • Date everything. Every claim shows when it was last reviewed and when sources were accessed.
  • Corrections are public. If we make an error, we correct it and timestamp the change on our Corrections page.
  • No conflicts of interest. Our reviewers disclose all potential conflicts. See our Sponsorship Disclosure.

Limitations

Cannabis research faces significant barriers: federal restrictions, limited funding, small sample sizes, and publication bias toward positive results. Our evidence grades reflect what is knowable given current research constraints. A "C" grade today does not mean a claim is false — it means the research isn't there yet. We update as the evidence evolves.
