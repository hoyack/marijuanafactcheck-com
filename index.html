How it works

Evidence grading, not opinion

Each claim passes through a structured evaluation: source quality, replication status, effect size, and conflict-of-interest screening. The result is a letter grade and a confidence level you can verify.

Evidence grades

What the letters mean

GradeLabelStandard
AStrong evidenceMultiple high-quality RCTs or systematic reviews with consistent results
BModerate evidenceAt least one RCT or multiple well-controlled observational studies
CLimited evidenceObservational studies, case series, or preclinical data only
DInsufficient evidenceConflicting studies, no controlled data, or anecdotal reports only
FContradictedStrong evidence against the claim; multiple studies refute it
